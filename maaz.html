What is Air Pollution?

Air pollution occurs when harmful substances like chemicals, dust, and smoke mix with the air we breathe. This makes the air toxic for humans, animals, and the entire environment. Today, it is a massive global crisis. Many major cities like Delhi and Beijing often face "emergency" levels of air quality where the air becomes visible as thick, grey smog.

Causes of Air Pollution

  • Vehicle Exhaust: Millions of cars and trucks release carbon gases every second.
  • Industrial Smoke: Factories pouring untreated smoke into the atmosphere.
  • Burning of Fossil Fuels: Power plants burning coal for electricity.
  • Garbage Burning: Releasing toxic fumes from plastic and waste.
  • Construction Dust: Fine particles from building sites that linger in the air.

Types of Air Pollutants

PM2.5 & PM10: Tiny particles that can enter deep into our lungs.

Carbon Monoxide (CO): An odorless gas from car exhausts that reduces oxygen in blood.

Sulphur Dioxide (SO2): Produced by factories; a major cause of acid rain.

Nitrogen Oxides (NOx): Contributes to the formation of smog and respiratory issues.

Effects of Air Pollution

Human Health

Leads to Asthma, chronic lung diseases, heart problems, and constant eye irritation.

Environment

Causes Global Warming, creates Acid Rain, and severely damages food crops.

Animals

Causes breathing difficulties for wildlife and leads to habitat destruction.

Air Pollution in India

In India, air pollution has become a seasonal disaster. During winters, northern India suffers from heavy smog due to stubble burning and low wind speeds. AQI (Air Quality Index) levels often cross the 400+ mark, which is "Hazardous." This environment is particularly dangerous for growing children and the elderly.

Prevention & Solutions

We must act now to ensure a breathable future. Here is how:

  • Use public transport or carpooling to reduce vehicles.
  • Plant more trees in your neighborhood.
  • Avoid burning household waste or dry leaves.
  • Switch to renewable energy like solar and wind.
  • Maintain your vehicles with regular PUC checks.
  • Spread awareness among friends and family.

What YOU can do:

Small steps like switching off lights when not needed or using a bicycle for short distances can make a huge difference.
